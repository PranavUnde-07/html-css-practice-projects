Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Scientist • Educator • 11th President of India

Short Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India (2002–2007). He is widely remembered for his role in India's civilian space program and military missile development, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India." He also inspired millions as an educator and author.

Key Achievements

  • Major contributor to India's civilian space program and ballistic missile development
  • Director of DRDO and key roles at ISRO
  • 11th President of India (2002–2007)
  • Author of popular books including Wings of Fire and Ignited Minds
  • Recipient of India's top civilian honours including the Bharat Ratna

Favourite Quote

"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action." — Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Why he matters

Dr. Kalam's life — from humble beginnings in Rameswaram to the Rashtrapati Bhavan — is a strong example of perseverance, scientific curiosity, and service to the nation. His encouragement of students and young innovators continues to be celebrated each year on his birthday (World Students' Day).
